RTP stands for Real-time Transport Protocol. 
It is a network protocol designed for delivering audio and video over IP networks.
RTP is widely used in communication and entertainment systems that involve streaming media, such as video conferencing, VoIP (Voice over IP), and live television broadcasts.

Feature of RTP

-> Transport of real-time data such as audio and video
-> Handles the actual transmission of media data
-> Transport protocol
-> No Session Control, focuses on the delivery of media streams
-> Typically runs over UDP
-> No commands; data transport only
-> Binary protocol with headers for synchronization and ordering
-> Relies on RTCP for error and control feedback
-> Typically uses dynamic ports, often starting from 5004 for RTP streams
-> Often used with RTCP for monitoring and feedback
